12.3 Making a boxplot

A boxplot (sometimes called a box and whiskers plot) displays the distribution of a continous variable. An individual boxplot divides a distribution into quartiles and visualizes them in a standardized way. For more information about the âanatomyâ of a boxplot, see: https://clauswilke.com/dataviz/boxplots-violins.html#fig:boxplot-schematic
